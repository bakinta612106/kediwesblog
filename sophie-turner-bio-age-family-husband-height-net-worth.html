Sophie Turner Biography

Sophie Turner is an English actress best known for her role as Sansa Stark in HBO’s epic fantasy television series Game of Thrones, which she played from 2011 until 2019. She was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 2019 for this role.

In 2013, she starred in the television series The Thirteenth Tale and made her feature film debut in Another Me. Turner starred in the animated comedy Barely Lethal in 2015, and from 2016 until 2019, she played a teenage Jean Gray/Phoenix in the X-Men film series.

In August 2009, Sophie was cast as Sansa Stark, a young noblewoman in HBO’s fantasy drama series Game of Thrones. Sansa was her first television role, and she began filming in July 2010 when she was 14 years old. Her work as Sansa, with her on-screen sister, Maisie Williams, earned her a nomination for the Young Artist Award for Best Performance in a TV Series—Supporting Young Actress in 2012. Turner appeared in all eight seasons of the television show.

In 2013, she made her film debut as the lead character in Another Me, an independent thriller based on Catherine MacPhail’s novel of the same name. In the 2013 television film The Thirteenth Tale, she played Adeline March. She was also cast in the comedy picture Barely Lethal, which was released in limited cinemas and on video on demand on May 29, 2015, the same year.
