Jules Sebastian reveals how to make sticky pork belly bites in the air fryer

Television presenter and fashion stylist Jules Sebastian has shown off her recipe for sticky pork belly bites and they take just 20 minutes in an air fryer.

The 40-year-old mother-of-two said the bites are not only crispy but very delicious – and something she plans on making again.

Jules’ video recipe went viral immediately with more than 80,000 people tuning in to see how she made the delicious bites.

‘I wish I had got a bigger piece of pork belly. Please try this,’ she wrote alongside the video. 

The popular home cook said she originally scrolled past the recipe on Instagram and knew she needed to recreate it at home immediately.

The recipe calls for a large piece of boneless pork belly, brown sugar, paprika, flour, garlic and onion.

For the sticky sauce you will need butter, golden syrup, bourbon, barbecue sauce and siracha. 

Jules begins her video by showing off the crispy, golden, bite-sized pieces of meat to the camera.

She then explains that the meat needs to be cut into small cubes and that the herbs, spices and flour needs to be mixed together in a bowl. 

She then coats the meat with the flour mixture before putting it uncovered in the fridge for two hours to ‘let it dry out’.

How can I make Jules’ pork belly nnites at home?

INGREDIENTS

1kg piece boneless pork belly

1 tbsp brown sugar

3 tsp smoked paprika

2 tsp plain flour

1 tsp onion powder

1/2 tsp garlic powder

Olive oil spray

 FOR THE SAUCE:

50g butter

2 tbsp golden syrup

2 tbsp barbecue sauce

1 tbsp bourbon

2 tsp sriracha

METHOD

Mix your brown sugar, paprika, flour, onion powder and garlic powder in a bowl. Slice your pork belly into 1cm cubes and spray with oil.

Coat the meat with the spice mix and then leave, uncovered, in the fridge for two hours.

This will help the meat dry out so it ends up being crispy.

Once the meat has been in tthe fridge it can be cooked.

Jules placed hers in an air fryer at 200C for 20 minutes.  

While the meat is cooking the sauce can be prepared.

Heat the butter, golden syrup and sauces in a pan, add the bourbon and cook until sticky.

Coat the meat with the sauce and serve with preferred sides.
